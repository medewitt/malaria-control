# Malaria Intervention Options: Descriptions and Impacts

This table outlines various malaria intervention strategies, their technical descriptions, and their estimated impacts on disease incidence and mortality.

| Intervention Options | Description | Estimated Impact on Disease Incidence | Source |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RTS,S/AS01 (Mosquirix)** | Subunit virus-like particle vaccine targeting the PfCSP antigen; administered as a 3-dose or 4-dose schedule for children. | Reduced clinical malaria by ~36% over 48 months in phase III testing; reduced all-cause childhood mortality by 13% in phase IV testing; seasonal administration reduced clinical malaria by 62.8% [1]. A 3-dose schedule (90% coverage) is estimated to avert a median of 93,940 clinical cases and 394 deaths per 100,000 children [2]. A 4-dose schedule (72% coverage for the 4th dose) is estimated to avert a median of 116,480 clinical cases and 484 deaths per 100,000 children in regions with $PfPR_{2–10}$ of 10–65% [2]. | 1-3 |
| **Insecticide-treated nets (ITNs) / LLINs** | Personal protection bed nets treated with insecticide (pyrethroids) to create a barrier and kill/repel mosquitoes during host-seeking. | Reduced clinical malaria episodes by 50%, parasite prevalence by 13%, and child mortality by 17% [3]. Associated with a 68% decrease in incidence [3]. Reduced all-cause death of children under 5 by 20% [4]. Estimated at $27 per DALY averted [2]. | 2-4 |
| **Indoor Residual Spraying (IRS)** | Coating interior walls and other surfaces with a residual insecticide to kill mosquitoes that rest on them after feeding. | Associated with 10-50 fold reductions in mosquito populations and similar declines in incidence [5]. Reduced the risk of malaria by 65% in meta-analysis [3]. Estimated to contribute to 10% of cases averted in Africa [5, 6]. Estimated at $143 per DALY averted [2]. | 2, 3, 5, 6 |
| **R21/Matrix-M** | CSP-based virus-like particle vaccine combined with Matrix-M adjuvant; recommended for children in high-transmission settings. | Reduced clinical malaria over 12 months by approximately 75% under seasonal administration or 68% under standard administration [1, 3]. | 1, 3 |
| **Artemether-lumefantrine (AL)** | Artemisinin-based combination therapy (ACT); lumefantrine has a shorter half-life than piperaquine but better gametocytocidal effects. | Reduces duration of infectiousness more than 50-fold compared to untreated infections [7]. Estimated 22% decrease in incidence attributed to ACT programs [3]. | 3, 7 |
| **Dihydroartemisinin-piperaquine (DHA-PQP)** | ACT where piperaquine provides a longer post-treatment prophylactic period (approx. 26-29 days). | Estimated 12% of cases averted compared to AL in moderate-to-high transmission areas in children; averted up to 100 cases per 1,000 people over 5 years in high-transmission seasonal settings [7]. | 3, 7 |
| **Artesunate (Injectable/Intravenous)** | Water-soluble derivative of artemisinin, recommended as the first-line treatment for severe malaria. | Decreased mortality of children in Africa by 22.5% compared with quinine treatment [3]. Contributes to a faster decline in mortality rates relative to case incidence (up to 40% mortality reduction when combined with other tools) [8]. | 3, 8 |
| **PfSPZ Vaccine** | Radiation-attenuated P. falciparum whole-sporozoite vaccine administered via direct venous inoculation. | Conferred 75-88% field efficacy in Malian adults when optimized; induced sterile hepatic immunity in malaria-naive recipients [1]. | 1, 3 |
| **Seasonal Malaria Chemoprevention (SMC)** | Administration of SP plus amodiaquine for children aged 6 months to 5 years (can be expanded to age 10). | Part of integrated strategies predicted to avert 1.7 billion cases and millions of deaths over 15 years in combination with other tools [8]. | 8 |
| **Accelerate 1** | Scale up vector control (LLINs) and ACT treatment to 80% coverage; switch to injectable artesunate for severe cases. | 21% reduction in case incidence and 40% reduction in mortality rates by 2030 compared with 2015 levels. | 8 |
| **Accelerate 2** | 90% coverage for LLINs and ACTs; include rectal artesunate for community-level pre-referral treatment. | 59% reduction in case incidence and 74% reduction in mortality rates by 2030 compared with 2015 levels. | 8 |
| **Innovate** | Accelerate 2 plus longer-lasting nets (4-year half-life) and expansion of SMC up to age 10. | 74% reduction in case incidence and 81% reduction in mortality rates by 2030 compared with 2015 levels. | 8 |
